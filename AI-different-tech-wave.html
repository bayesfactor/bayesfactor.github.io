Is This Time Different?

Will AI and Robotics be yet another technology wave like past waves of automation where people shift jobs but the economic unemployment remains low, or is this time different? What do serious thinkers and forecasting aggregates say on this topic? Horizontal: will AI/robotics break the historical "displace → reabsorb" job pattern across the economy? Vertical: will the outcome be bad for typical workers?

Hover a point for its reasoning and source — or tap on touchscreens. Click a point to open details.

How to read the numbers. Each value is a probability, so "20" means 20:80. Different? = P(AI/robotics produces a structural break or persistent technological unemployment within ~10–15 yr). Bad for workers? = P(net-negative outcome for typical workers: mass displacement and/or sharply falling labor share with no adequate offset).

These are interpretive calibrations of each thinker's stated position, not probabilities they have personally quoted — imagine error bars of ±15. The exceptions are the aggregate rows, which report real elicited figures. Two of those (AI Impacts ESPAI, Metaculus/Manifold) speak only to the capability/timeline axis, so they appear in a separate rail beneath the plot — same horizontal scale, but deliberately outside the worker-outcome axis, since they make no claim there.
